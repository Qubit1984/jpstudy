## Sentence pattern：V2 + あがる 【intransitive】

indicates that a certain action has been thoroughly completed. Since it is an intransitive verb, it refers to the completed state of an object.

<Example
  ja="ピザが焼きあがりました。"
  en="The pizza is fully baked.(intransitive verb)"
  src="/jlptN3/1-1.mp3"
/>
<br />
<Example
  ja="洋服が仕上がりました。"
  en="The dress is fully completed.(intransitive verb)"
  src="/jlptN3/1-2.mp3"
/>
<br />
<Example
  ja="原稿が書きあがりました。"
  en="The manuscript is fully written.(intransitive verb)"
  src="/jlptN3/1-3.mp3"
/>
Additionally, when referring to the rain stopping in Japanese, common expressions
are 「雨が止む」 and「雨が上がる」「雨が止んだ」 simply means the rain has stopped,
but it may start again (uncertain). However, if you say「雨が上がった」, it indicates
that the rain has completely (thoroughly) stopped and it has cleared up.

## Sentence pattern：V2 + あげる 【transitive verb】

indicates thoroughly completing a certain action. Since it is a transitive verb, it refers to an action that someone has completed.

<Example
  ja="専門家の評価によると、その技術は非常に有望だと言われています。"
  en="I have finished baking the pizza.(transitive verb)"

/>
